Android VCO Based Voltage Meter

Hack any headphone jack credit card reader into a voltmeter

At a seminar, the professor challenged their audience of teachers to think of ways to incorporate student smartphones in curriculums. I created a simple circuit and design an Android App that uses the headphone jack as a means of obtaining input.

I used an LM231 voltage-to-frequency converter, that created a square wave proportional to the input voltage. This squarewave was that is sampled and frequency determined and converted back to a voltage.

At first the algorithm used an FFT, but the strategy of measuring the square-wave's zero-crossing proved faster and more reliable.

Automated Gardening

Garden Automation, Cloud-Connected Sensors, Aquaponics

To gain an intuition about plants before embarking on delicate aquaponics systems, I built a setup which logged light levels, soil moisture level, and temperature so that I could start running experiments.

I started with varying the light level, and taking pictures once a week during watering (which I used an erlenmeyer flask to measure to a precise amount each week).

I pushed the data to the cloud for remote monitoring, using an Electric Imp to stream the raw analog data.

On the server, a moving average was performed on soil-moisture-data (since this was particularly noisy).

Illuminance data, and pH data, revealing interesting interplays -- most notably that some plants appeared to "drink" more just when the lights turned off, namely: data consistenly has shown a sharp decline in soil moisture level for the Sanseveria independent of temperature and time of day that I program the LEDs to shutdown.

FoodRev/UrBonFood

ML and Mobile Apps for

The foodRev (previously UrBonFood) App is a project rapidly iterating mobile-app/AI-forward-planner/node.js-server that does heavy lifting and answers critical questions (where food is needed, where food is in excess, and a means to connect them) assisting our teams of volunteers to deliver nutritious meals to shelters, food-pantries, and community centers.

Realizing that this had the potential to make a huge impact if polished, I did the responsible thing and reached out to SWE, and Data Science and ML experts in an appeal to create an App and logistics planner with impact as far as mobile.

We're going to be seeing a major overhaul in our web-design, IOS and Android Apps thanks to volunteers from GoogleServe as well as SF Startups, utilizing an AI-based Forward Planner hand-crafted by Principle Data Scientist of the AT&T Big Data Group and Udacity's Data Science Instructor Don Dini.
